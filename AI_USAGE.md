# Generative AI Usage

This project makes extensive use of generative AI in its development.

## Code

The large majority of this codebase — including the Three.js scene setup, the
cannon-es physics integration, the bike controller, input handling, the
terrain/route data pipeline (`tools/terrain/`), and project documentation — was
written with [Claude Sonnet 5](https://www.anthropic.com/claude), an AI model
by [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/), operated through
[Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code). Commits and pull requests
produced this way are attributed to Claude in the commit trailers and PR
descriptions where applicable.

All AI-assisted contributions are reviewed and merged at the discretion of the
project's human maintainer. AI involvement doesn't change the terms of the
[MIT License](LICENSE) that covers this repository's code.

## Non-code assets

The bike model is a pre-existing CC-BY-licensed asset (not AI-generated) —
see [`ATTRIBUTION.md`](ATTRIBUTION.md) for its source and licence. The ground
texture and audio are currently generated procedurally in-browser at runtime,
not by generative AI. `ATTRIBUTION.md` also lists candidate real-asset sources
for the future, including a paid generative AI option (ElevenLabs) that isn't
integrated yet.

## Terrain & route data

The Cut Gate terrain and route data are derived from real Environment Agency
LIDAR and OpenStreetMap data, not generated by AI — see
[`ATTRIBUTION.md`](ATTRIBUTION.md) and
[`tools/terrain/README.md`](tools/terrain/README.md).
